Nilagang Baboy

Nilagang Baboy is a Filipino pork soup made by simmering pork (typically ribs or shoulder) with a variety of vegetables like corn, potatoes, and leafy greens in a clear, savory broth, offering a light yet flavorful dish.

Nilagang Baboy

2 lbs pork ribs 8-10 cups of water 1 pork bouillon cube 1 medium size onion, quartered 1 tbsp whole peppercorns 2 potatoes, quartered 1/2 head cabbage, sliced 1 small bundle bokchoy / pechay 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)

Adobo

Adobo is a quintessential Filipino dish made by marinating meat (usually chicken or pork) in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, then simmering it until tender, creating a rich, tangy, and savory flavor.

Adobo

3/4 cup soy sauce 1/2 cup sugar 6 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed 2 bay leaves 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns 3 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs (about 8 pieces) 1/4 cup canola oil 1 large yellow onion, finely diced 1/2 cup chicken broth 1/2 cup cane vinegar Steamed rice, for serving

Sinigang

Sinigang is a popular Filipino sour soup made with a variety of meats (such as pork, shrimp, or fish) and vegetables, simmered in a tangy broth flavored with tamarind, giving it a refreshing and savory taste.

Sinigang

6 cups water 1 kg MONTEREY PORK BELLY, sinigang cut 6 pc tomatoes, quartered 1 (44 g) sachet sinigang mix 3 pc gabi, peeled and cut into cubes 1 bundle sitaw, cut into 1 inch pieces 1 pc labanos, sliced 4 pc okra iodized fine salt or patis to taste 1 bundle kangkong, use tender leaves and stalks only (cut stalks into 1" pieces)
